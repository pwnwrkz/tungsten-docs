Once you’ve mastered basic syncing and asset packing, you can start leveraging Tungsten’s power-user features to turn your asset management into a hands-off, automated pipeline.

This guide covers how to keep your workspace live, how to handle high-resolution assets, and how to integrate Tungsten into your CI/CD process.

## Watch Mode

`tungsten watch` is the core of a modern developer's loop. Instead of manually running `sync` every time you make a small change, Tungsten keeps a persistent connection to your project directory.

Any modification to your assets. Adding an icon, updating a texture, or tweaking a sprite, triggers an immediate, incremental sync. This keeps your local development environment perfectly in sync with the Roblox cloud.

### Usage
Start the watcher in your terminal:
```bash title="Terminal"
tungsten watch cloud
```

:::note
This command will block your terminal until you stop it (usually by pressing Ctrl + C).
:::

## High DPI Detection

Tungsten is built to handle high-resolution displays out of the box. You don't need to enable any specific settings; Tungsten automatically inspects your asset filenames for standard high-resolution suffixes (like @2x or @3x).

When Tungsten detects these, it automatically generates a function for changing between DPI scales in your Luau code. This ensures your images remain crisp and properly scaled on modern displays without requiring any extra configuration from you.

## CI/CD Integration

For teams or larger projects, you shouldn't be syncing assets from your personal machine. Tungsten is designed to be CI-ready.

By providing the `TUNGSTEN_GLOBAL_APIKEY` environment variable in your GitHub Actions or GitLab CI runners, you can automate your asset pipeline entirely.

<Steps>

1. Define `TUNGSTEN_GLOBAL_APIKEY` as a secret in your repository settings.
2. Add a step to your workflow to run tungsten sync cloud.
3. Use the `--dry-run` flag first to validate that your asset configuration is valid without consuming your upload quota.

</Steps>

:::tip
Pro Workflow: Using `--dry-run` in your CI pipeline allows you to validate your packing logic and file structure on every push to your repository, ensuring no broken assets ever hit your production environment.
:::
